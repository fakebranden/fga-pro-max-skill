## Do

- Lead the hero with a wide shot of the truck *in scene*, not a comp render. Show the awning open, baristas working, customers in frame. Sets every other expectation.
- Render the current week's location(s) at the top of the page as a live card — date + city + time window. This is the #1 search reason for this niche.
- Make "Book the truck for my event" the primary above-the-fold CTA. This is the high-margin revenue, not walk-up sales.
- Show event pricing tiers (or starting price) on the booking page. Couples planning weddings disqualify themselves if no price anchor exists.
- Photograph close-up product (the cup, the cone, the plate) as a secondary hero strip — the truck is the macro shot, the product is the micro.
- Include a "What we serve" menu with photo per item, not text-only list. Drink trucks especially live or die on the product photography.
- Surface event types served: weddings, corporate, school, festival, birthday. Each gets one line + one photo. AEO targets each query directly.
- Show the route / schedule for the next 4 weeks — calendar grid or simple list. Operators in this niche rotate quickly and "where will you be" is a daily search.

## Never

- Render the truck only as the logo. The actual photograph of the truck IS the brand. A vector outline is not a substitute.
- "Order online" CTA above "Book the truck." Reverses the value of the page — walk-up sales are nice, events are the business.
- Stock cafeteria / catering photos. Trucks have a specific visual register — outdoor, daylit, neighborhood — that stock libraries can't fake.
- Static Google Maps embed showing one location. Trucks move; use a route list or "this week's stops" card instead.
- "Coming soon" pages with the truck obscured. If the truck exists, photograph it. If it doesn't, the operator isn't ready to ship a site.

## Typography mood

Display: a bold geometric or slab serif (Anton, Bebas Neue, Cooper Bold) — should evoke vintage signage without being kitsch. Body: a friendly humanist sans (Inter, Manrope) at slightly larger size than typical (16-18px body) — these pages are often scanned outdoors on phones. Optional accent: a small chalkboard-style script ONLY for "Today's special" type ephemera, never for primary headlines. Pair with `blue-bottle-coffee` for the disciplined craft register or `sweetgreen` for the bright outdoor palette.

## Key effect

A "live" location card at the top — pulled from a Google Sheet, a CMS, or even a hardcoded weekly update — that renders "This week: {city}, {dates}, {time}." The card is high-contrast, mobile-thumb-sized, and sits between the hero photograph and the booking CTA. It is the single piece of content that returning visitors come back for. Treat it like a dashboard widget, not a sentence in a paragraph.

## Copy patterns

- "Where's the {truck name} this week? {City}, {dates}."
- "Book the truck for your {wedding | corporate event | school day | block party}."
- "{N} drinks per hour. Pricing starts at {price}."
- "Serving {neighborhood} every {weekday morning}."
- "We bring the truck. You bring the guests."
- "Next stop: {location}, {date}."
- "Catered {N}+ weddings in {year}."
- "{Signature drink/dish} — only on the truck."

## CTA patterns

- "Book the truck"
- "See this week's locations"
- "Get a wedding quote"
- "Reserve for a corporate event"
- "Check next week's schedule"

## Anti-patterns (niche-specific)

- A "menu" page with no photos. Food and drink trucks live on visual product — every menu item needs a photo, even a phone snap.
- Wedding gallery without per-photo location context. "Smith Wedding, Tampa, May 2024" beats anonymous photo grids.
- An e-commerce cart for merch above the event-booking section. Merch is a footer link, not a hero feature.
- "We come to you" buried in body copy. It needs to be the H1 sub or a section heading on its own.
- A "Follow us on Instagram" hero swap-in. The truck rolling up is the hero — IG is the bottom rail.

## Reference token affinity

- `blue-bottle-coffee` — the disciplined craft palette + restrained type works for coffee/drink trucks (Travelin' Tom's reference build).
- `sweetgreen` — bright outdoor-daylight register fits food trucks with a healthy / festival positioning.
- `fga-canonical` — yellow accent reads well against the photographs of trucks in scene; works as the booking CTA pop.
