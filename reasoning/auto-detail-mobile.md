## Do

- Build a real before/after slider with at least 6 jobs: paint correction, ceramic coating, interior shampoo, headlight restoration. Each labeled with vehicle make + service.
- Make the booking form vehicle-aware on the first field: "Sedan, SUV, Truck, RV, Boat." Price varies by vehicle and the question filters seriously interested leads.
- Surface "We come to you" in the H1 sub or as a hero pill. Mobile is the differentiator vs. a fixed-location wash; lead with it.
- Render a service-area map or zip-code radius ("Serving {city} + 25 miles"). Out-of-area visitors should self-disqualify on the home page.
- Show service tiers with anchor pricing: "Express wash from $X, Full detail from $Y, Ceramic coat from $Z." Hidden pricing kills the call.
- Include water + power requirements honestly — "We bring water and power; just need driveway space." Common buyer question, answer it on the page.
- Photograph the work van / mobile rig in scene. The rig is a credibility signal — it shows the operator is set up to actually do the work.
- Include an "RVs / boats / commercial fleet" line item with photo. Higher-margin segment that's invisible without a dedicated mention.

## Never

- Stock photo of a clean red sports car on a white background. Show actual customer cars, actual addresses (blurred plates), actual results.
- A pricing table with no "starting at" — fixed prices mislead because vehicle + condition vary. Use anchors + "Get exact quote" CTAs.
- Video of soap bubbles in slow motion. Cliché. Show paint correction progress shots or ceramic-coat water-beading clips.
- "Eco-friendly" copy without specifics. Either name the products + practices (rinseless wash, biodegradable soap) or omit the claim.
- A "Membership" or "Subscription" hero before the booking is offered. Subscriptions are upsells AFTER the first wash, not the lead.

## Typography mood

Display: a tight technical sans (Eurostile, Rajdhani, Barlow, Geist Mono for accent labels). Body: a clean utility sans (Inter, IBM Plex Sans). Palette: black + white + chrome/silver + one saturated accent (orange or electric blue tends to pop against dark photography). Headlines in upper case or sentence case — never script. Pair with `linear` for the disciplined-technical register or `fga-canonical` for the yellow-on-black contrast against dark vehicle photography.

## Key effect

Before/after slider showing real water-beading on a freshly ceramic-coated hood, or a swirl-mark removal in real paint. Same lighting, same angle, same vehicle — drag handle in the middle. Place the booking form (vehicle type → zip → service tier) directly below the slider so the visual proof flows into the conversion form. The combination of "I can see it work" + "answer 3 questions to book" is the entire page in one scroll.

- "Get a quote for my vehicle"
- "Schedule a ceramic coat"
- "Add my truck or RV"
- "Reserve a same-day slot"

## Anti-patterns (niche-specific)

- A 12-step process diagram. Detail customers want price + when + done — not your wash methodology.
- Instagram embed as the gallery. The gallery is curated, captioned, and lives on the site. IG is a footer link.
- "Loyalty card" upsell on the home page. Save for the post-booking flow.
- A "Why ceramic" educational essay above the booking form. Education goes BELOW the conversion form, not above.
- A static price PDF download. Pricing renders inline, on the page, in HTML.
