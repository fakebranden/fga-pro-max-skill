## Do

- Photograph appliances on white seamless or clean-room cyclorama. Crisp, even lighting. No props, no kitchen scene behind.
- Surface install + delivery + haul-away as first-class trust chips on the hero — not buried in an FAQ. These are the top-three queries.
- Render brand logos for the brands carried (LG, Samsung, GE, Whirlpool, etc.) as a strip. Permission-cleared brand logos are powerful trust signals in this niche.
- Show financing options clearly ("0% APR for 12 months" or partner like Affirm/Klarna). Appliance shoppers often qualify on financing.
- Include a warranty table with terms in plain English. AEO crawlers extract these for "warranty terms" queries.
- Show inventory state honestly — "In stock," "Order in 3-5 days," "Sold out, joining waitlist." Live inventory beats static catalog every time.
- Include before/after delivery photos in the gallery — the old unit out, the new unit in. Sells the white-glove story.
- Pin a "Get pricing" or "Check availability" CTA on every product card. Open-loop pricing converts better than displayed price on used inventory.

## Never

- Lifestyle photography of families using appliances. Stock-feel + trust collapses immediately. Show the products clean and product-y.
- "Best prices in town" copy. Unsubstantiated, AEO-irrelevant, and reads bait-and-switch.
- Hidden delivery fees. State the fee or state "free over $X" — concealed cost is the #1 review complaint in this niche.
- A 12-tab nav for every product category. Use a clear 4-6 category grid on the home page and let category pages handle depth.
- "About Us" hero featuring the storefront. The storefront photo belongs in the location section; the hero is for product or trust.

## Typography mood

Display: a clean industrial sans (Inter, IBM Plex Sans, Geist) at heavy weight on headlines. Body: same family, lighter weight, generous line-height. This niche is competing visually with Best Buy / Home Depot / Lowe's — restraint and clarity beat character. Pair with `linear` for the tech-retail palette discipline or `apple` for the clean-product photography register.

## Key effect

The trust-chip overlay. Three small horizontal chips sitting on the hero photograph — "Free delivery," "Same-day install," "10-year warranty" — render as the first thing the eye picks up after the product. They are NOT in a separate row below the hero; they're layered onto it. This single move differentiates a serious appliance retailer from an online catalog dump.

## Copy patterns

- "{Brand} {category} starting at {price} — install included."
- "Stocked in {city} today, install by {weekday}."
- "We take the old one when we deliver the new one."
- "{N}-year warranty, in writing, on every unit."
- "0% financing for {N} months on {category}."
- "Same-day delivery within {N} miles of {neighborhood}."
- "Replacement {category} for under ${price}."
- "Licensed installers, insured, in {state}."

## CTA patterns

- "Check availability"
- "Get a delivery quote"
- "Book the install"
- "See financing options"
- "Get a haul-away quote"

## Anti-patterns (niche-specific)

- A 4-step "Configure your appliance" wizard on the home page. Most users want to see what's in stock, not a quiz.
- Hero video of a washing machine spinning. Static product shot every time.
- "Compare to the big-box stores" call-out without a price screenshot. Either cite or omit.
- Reviews section with 50+ generic 5-star quotes. Pull 3-5 specific reviews mentioning install / delivery / warranty by name.
- A "Repair services" section conflated with retail. If the operator also repairs, give it a separate page; mixing the two confuses both intents.

- "{legal_entity}: Thanks for your purchase. Your {N}-year warranty is active starting today. Save this number for service requests. Reply STOP to opt out, HELP for help. You opted in by submitting our form."

## Reference token affinity

- `linear` — disciplined neutral palette + Inter type registers as a serious tech-retail brand.
- `apple` — for higher-end appliance positioning where the product photography is the brand.
- `stripe` — works when the financing story needs to read fintech-credible.
