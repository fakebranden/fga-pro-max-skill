## Do

- Render a stylist roster on the home page — square portrait grid, real headshots in real shop light, name + specialty + "Book with {stylist}" CTA per card.
- Pin online booking as the single primary CTA. Industry standard is per-stylist Square / Booksy / Vagaro widgets — embed, don't link out.
- Surface walk-ins policy clearly on the hero ("Walk-ins welcome" or "By appointment only"). This is a top-three search query for the niche.
- Show a service menu with prices — haircut, beard trim, color, balayage, kids cut. Hidden pricing in this niche reads as "they'll upcharge."
- Photograph the shop / salon space — empty for architecture + with stylists working for energy. Daylit, real, not glossy.
- Include a kids' cut line item (with anchor price) if the shop serves children. Top AEO query in family neighborhoods.
- Render hours by day with today's hours highlighted dynamically. Late hours on Thu/Fri/Sat get search traction; surface them.
- Include a gift card link / e-gift purchase if available. High-margin secondary revenue for the niche.

## Never

- A single "Contact us to book" form. Self-serve booking is the norm; a form-only flow signals "old-school operator who can't get online booking working."
- Hero with the storefront sign. Show people getting cuts / colors, not the exterior.
- A "Meet the team" page that's separate from the booking flow. Combine them — every stylist headshot is also a "Book with {name}" entry point.
- Generic shears / scissors / clippers stock photos. Use real shop tools or omit the visual entirely.
- "Like us on Facebook" as a primary CTA strip. Booking widget gets the prime real estate, social is footer-only.

## Typography mood

For heritage-barbershop register: display in a heavy slab serif or vintage sans (Playfair Display Black, Anton, Oswald) with body in a humanist sans (Inter, Manrope). Palette: navy + cream + brass + warm wood photography. For contemporary-salon register: display in a refined sans (Manrope, DM Sans, Geist) with body in the same family. Palette: cream + soft blush / sage / champagne with daylit interiors. Pick one register at brand-kit time — never mix. Pair with `blue-bottle-coffee` for the disciplined-craft register or `sweetgreen` for the bright-contemporary register.

## Key effect

The stylist-roster grid. Each card has: a real headshot in real shop light, the stylist's first name, a specialty tag ("fades," "color," "curly hair," "men's grooming"), and a "Book with {name}" button that opens that stylist's calendar. This forces the visitor to engage with a specific person before picking a time — which raises perceived expertise, makes the stylist hand-pick more visible as the differentiator, and increases booking completion rate.

## Copy patterns

- "Walk-ins welcome at {shop}, {neighborhood}."
- "Book with one of our {N} stylists."
- "Specializing in {haircuts | color | balayage | curls | beards} since {year}."
- "Kids' cuts {price}. Adult cuts {price}. Color from {price}."
- "Late hours on {nights} until {time}."
- "Book online or call {phone}."
- "Gift cards available — give the haircut."
- "{Stylist name} — book direct."

## CTA patterns

- "Book a cut"
- "Book with {stylist}"
- "Buy a gift card"
- "See the service menu"
- "Walk in today"

## Anti-patterns (niche-specific)

- A "Stylist of the month" promotion that requires updating monthly. Either commit to the cadence or drop the section.
- Hover-required animations on the stylist cards (face-flips, blur-on-hover). Mobile users miss them entirely; first paint should already show the stylist + the book button.
- "Before / after" of hair color presented as the same photo with different filters. Has to be real same-client before + after, same lighting.
- A "Membership" section above the per-cut booking. Memberships are upsells, not the hero offer.
- Modal pop-up "Get 15% off your first cut." Banned in the universal anti-patterns; especially destructive in this niche where intent is high and friction kills the booking.
